School Introduction:

The Ottawa Catholic School Board Korean School was established in 1977 through the efforts of Korean immigrants. Over the years, with the dedication of many Korean school teachers, the school has grown its student body. Starting in 1991, the Ontario provincial government began providing funding based on the number of students to ensure a proper educational environment. As part of the International Language Program (ILP), Korean Credit Program was established to offer high school credits.


Since its establishment, the Ottawa Catholic School Board Korean School has been providing opportunities for Korean-Canadian children to learn the Korean language, culture, and history. It has contributed significantly to maintaining and developing the mother tongue of Korean immigrants, fostering Korean pride and identity.


Furthermore, it aims to foster global talents who can naturally understand and embrace both Korean and Canadian cultures, enabling them to demonstrate their abilities in any society in the future.




School Location:

In 2018, the school relocated and was renamed Ottawa Catholic School Board Korean School. The high school credit program operates according to the rules set by the Ontario Ministry of Education and is located at St. Mother Teresa High School in Barrhaven (440 Longfields Dr., Nepean, ON K2J 4T1), serving students in grades 9-12.

    • Credits:

    The credit program is divided into 3 levels for students in grades 9-12. Upon completion of this program, one credit is awarded each year, and upon completion of Level 3, a total of 3 credits are awarded.


    If you have proof documents such as transcripts or report cards from other schools, you can submit them for recognition of previously earned Korean language credits, allowing you to take courses at the recognized level.

    Checking Grades:

    The OCSB (Ottawa Catholic School Board): Midterm grades for the Korean classes in the IILP program are issued in January or February each year, and grade transcripts are issued in June.


    Each student should annually verify with their school's Guidance Counsellor to ensure the accuracy of all grades and records.


    In particular, 12th-grade students must consult with their Guidance Counsellor in November when applying for university admission regarding their Korean language (IILP Korean Credit Program) credits.
